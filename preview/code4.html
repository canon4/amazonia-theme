The Product Story

This Ceremonial Basket is more than a container; it is a repository of ancestral memory. Woven by hand using fibers from the Chambira Palm, each strand is harvested sustainably from the heart of the Colombian Amazon.

The Tikuna people, known as "The People of the Water," have perfected this weaving technique over centuries. The process begins with the extraction of the palm heart, followed by boiling the fibers with natural pigments derived from Achiote and Huito fruits to achieve the deep, earthy reds and blacks.

Every geometric pattern tells a story of the river's flow or the movement of sacred animals. When you hold this basket, you are holding weeks of patient craftsmanship and a connection to a landscape that provides everything needed for life.

In Afro-Amazonian and Indigenous cultures, these objects serve as spiritual anchors. The "Diamond" pattern featured on this basket represents the protective skin of the Anaconda, the mythical guardian of the Amazon's waters.

By choosing this product, you are actively participating in the preservation of the Tikuna language and traditional forest management practices that have kept the Amazon diverse for millennia.
